## 3. DOCTRINAL REASONING

### Primary Framework: Major Questions + Statutory Text

Kagan will anchor her reasoning in **textual specificity combined with major questions doctrine**, applying the framework from *West Virginia v. EPA* (which she joined in dissent but whose analytical structure she accepts). Three textual hooks matter most:

**1. "Regulate...importation" vs. explicit tariff language**
Kagan will emphasize the **expressio unius principle** from her opinions like *Food Marketing Institute v. Argus Leader* and *Connecticut National Bank v. Germain* ("when statutory language is unambiguous, judicial inquiry is complete"). Congress knows how to authorize tariffs explicitly—it did so in §232 ("adjust imports of an article and its derivatives"), Trade Act §122 (explicit percentage caps and time limits), and §301. IEEPA's silence on "tariffs," "duties," or "impose" is deliberate, especially given the 1977 enactment came **after** these other trade statutes.

**2. Structural canon against rendering §122 superfluous**
Like her analysis in *King v. Burwell* (where she joined Roberts's opinion refusing to "make a fortress out of the dictionary" but insisting on reading provisions together), Kagan will find it "implausible" that Congress enacted Trade Act §122's carefully calibrated 15% cap and 150-day limit for balance-of-payments emergencies while simultaneously authorizing **unlimited** tariffs under IEEPA for the same circumstances. The government's reading makes §122 "mere surplusage."

**3. Major questions doctrine application**
Kagan joined the dissent in *West Virginia v. EPA*, but she has accepted major questions analysis when properly grounded in statutory structure (see *Biden v. Nebraska*, where she dissented on application but not framework). Here, the "vast economic and political significance" is undeniable—$5.2 trillion over 10 years per the Wharton model. Kagan will cite her own *NFIB v. OSHA* concurrence emphasizing that Congress doesn't "hide elephants in mouseholes." IEEPA's general "regulate" language is the mousehole; unlimited global tariff authority is the elephant.
